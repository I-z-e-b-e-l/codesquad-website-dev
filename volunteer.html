Volunteer with CodeSquad

CodeSquad regularly needs volunteers with software development and human resources backgrounds to serve as mentors, coaches, speakers and panelists. Here are some ideas:

The Advocates Program

A private directory volunteer "Advocates" who meet with CodeSquad students or grads one-on-one for single session 30-60 minute video calls to provide support on things like:

  • Resume review
  • Portfolio review
  • Project support
  • Day in the life at their job / at their company
  • Technical interview prep and practice
  • Behavioral & technical interview prep and practice
  • General career advice
  • Advice/Discussion relating to being an underrepresented identity in tech, or being a person with a disability in tech, etc. (BIPOC, Queer, Trans, ADHD, etc.)
Meetings occur throughout the program lifecycle at whatever times are mutually convenient for the student/grad and the Advocate. If you hit it off, you're welcome to stay in touch!

The Graduate Mentor Program

CodeSquad's Graduate Mentor Program provides a framework for grads to connect with industry professionals as mentors and build a relationship that will improve persistence and success in steps towards getting a job and reaching career goals.
Mentor/Mentee meetings are held via video chat, once a week for the first month, typically starting in June or July, and approximately once a month for the following five months.
Mentors may assist mentees with:

  • Illuminating their career goals and outlining steps to reach them
  • Brainstorming solutions to barriers that may get in a graduate's way
  • Connections to professional networks, opportunities, and industry contacts
  • Help graduates develop employer desired “soft” skills, understand workplace expectations, and increase their likelihood of finding a great career.
  • Support to mitigate imposter syndrome
  • Support for graduates of marginalized identities in navigating racism, sexism, etc. in the tech industry

Career Specialization Panel Speakers

Throughout CodeSquad's program, we invite professionals from different specializations within the tech industry to visit and speak about their career path: How did you get into tech? What challenges did you face along the way? What do you love (and hate) about your job? What would a bootcamp grad have to learn if they wanted your job? etc. Our goal is to feature professionals of diversity, inspire students to continue learning, and illuminate what career paths can look like after CodeSquad.

We are most interested in inviting professionals of underrepresented identities from the following career paths:

  • Front end, back end, and full stack development
  • Freelance web development
  • AI and machine learning
  • Cybersecurity
  • Product/project management
  • Database technology
  • UI design and UX research
  • Tech recruiting

Networking Champions

Interview Practice Day

CodeSquad’s Interview Practice Day, pairs volunteer recruiters and hiring managers with individual CodeSquad students for a practice interview. Volunteers share feedback privately with students, and are invited to meet other volunteers and share feedback with CodeSquad staff at the end of the interview. Date TBA, Summer 2022.


Educational Champions
Help us teach a class, provide currculum feedback, develop a pair-programming exercise, or run a coding challenge. Your experience can really help.
